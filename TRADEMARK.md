# Trademark and Brand Policy

## No official affiliation

FanSafe is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or officially connected to
FIFA, any World Cup organizing body, any national football association, any
city or tourism authority, or any telecom/eSIM provider. Any football-event
or World Cup context in FanSafe's positioning refers to a **reference use
case**, not an official partnership. This is verified against the current
codebase: no FIFA/World Cup branding, wordmark, or trademarked term appears
anywhere in `FanSafe_PWA/index.html`, `manifest.json`, or any repository
document (checked by direct search of the source for "fifa" / "world cup" —
zero matches outside this trademark policy itself).

If FanSafe is ever deployed for a specific event or in partnership with an
organization, that partnership must be disclosed explicitly in the app and
in this file — never implied through visual styling or copy alone.

## "FanSafe" name and app icon

The name "FanSafe," the wordmark, and the app icons (`icon-192.png`,
`icon-512.png`) are **not** covered by the permissive MIT repository
license in `LICENSE`. This is intentional and standard practice: it
prevents a fork from presenting itself as "FanSafe" while diverging in ways
that could confuse users about safety-relevant behavior.

You may:

- Fork the code under its license terms and build your own product
- Refer to your fork as "based on FanSafe" or "a FanSafe fork," with a link
  back to this repository

You may not, without written permission from the maintainer:

- Distribute a modified version using the name "FanSafe" or a confusingly
  similar name
- Use the FanSafe icon or wordmark for a fork with materially different
  safety behavior (e.g., a fork that removes the no-false-claims wording
  around "sent"/"delivered" messages, or the medical-card hide-by-default
  behavior)
- Imply official FIFA, World Cup, government, or emergency-service
  endorsement using the FanSafe name or icon

## No registered trademark yet

No trademark registration exists for "FanSafe" as of this writing. This
document describes the maintainer's intended brand policy in advance of any
registration, not a legal trademark claim.
